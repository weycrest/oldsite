POP3/IMAP (incoming) Email and SMTP (outgoing) Mail Service

POP3 is probably the most well known of all the e-mail "protocols" in use and is probably how you download email at present. However POP3 only works well when used with one computer at a time, as e-mail is downloaded to individual computers and laptops so stored messages are not always accessible. If you have ever had to boot or login to another workstation just to grab some messages you will know what we mean.

IMAP email ensures email can be accessed and manipulated from multiple locations (at home, work and by webmail). "Authenticated" SMTP services allows you send mail out through our servers rather than your broadband or dialup provider, using your POP3 login credentials. Secure SSL enabled POP3, IMAP and SMTP is also supported for added security when accessing your mail in insecure locations or networks.
