Whats in a Domain Name?

There are two parts to a domain. The name itself, and the top level domain extension.

The most popular TLD is the .com (dot com) extension - synonymous with online business. But due to its popularity, getting the '.com' of your choice is getting harder.

TLDs orginally had a meaning behind them. Nets were originally used exclusively by network operators, and .orgs are supposed to be used by non-profit organizations. A domain with a TLD of 'co.uk' suggests a UK presence.

There are other TLDs such as .uk.com, .biz, and .tk. These are all associated with either a company or with a country.

Do you need a Web Host?

There are a number of ways to set up a "website." Firstly there are organisations that provide 'free' hosting accounts. Unsuprisingly, as they have earn some money somehow, there are some disadvantages. This is usually by advertising on your website, often in the form of pop-up or banner. This pretty much destroys the chance of presenting a professional image to customers if you are intending to sell online. Additionally you will be limited in the web space you can use have and the number email accounts (if they are provided at all). Often you will be prevented from using your own cgi or php scripts, which is pretty much a must have for dynamic content such as blogs or ecommerce carts.

The second option is for you to buy and install your own server (very expensive and very difficult). You would then need to buy a leased line connection either in your own premises or at a specialist centre (i.e. London TeleHouse). You could run it on an ADSL line but this is not a robust solution. A variation of this is to lease a 'dedicated server' where you are the only client using the server and it is maintained by the hosting company. But this can work out to be even more expensive than running your own.

So the more common purchase a "virtual" or shared web hosting package from a professional Web hosting provider. Choosing a Web hosting company is a very personal decision based on your own unique needs. Switching web hosting companies is a painful process that you'd only wish on your worst enemies. So try to choose a company that not only meets your needs today, but can also grow with you as you expand.
